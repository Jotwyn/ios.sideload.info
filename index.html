What is Sideloading?

Sideloading is the process of installing apps on your iOS device from sources outside the official App Store. This allows you to use apps that are not available on the App Store, including betas, open-source projects, emulators, and custom or legacy apps.

Is Sideloading Legal?

Sideloading is legal in most countries, including the United States and EU, as long as you are not using it to install pirated or malicious software. However, some apps or services may have their own terms of use, and Apple may restrict certain sideloading methods in future iOS updates.

What is an IPA file?

An IPA file (iOS App Store Package) is the file format used to package and distribute iOS apps. It is similar to an APK file on Android. You will need IPA files to sideload apps onto your device.

What can I do once I can sideload?

  • Install apps not available on the App Store (emulators, open-source tools, legacy apps, etc.)
  • Test beta versions of apps or your own development projects
  • Use alternative app stores or app managers
  • Access features or customizations not allowed by Apple

What should I know before starting?

  • Backup your device: It is strongly recommended to backup your device to iCloud, iTunes, or Finder (on macOS Catalina or newer) in case of data loss.
  • Keep your device charged: Make sure your device is plugged in or has sufficient battery to avoid unexpected shutdowns during sideloading.
  • Data loss is rare: Sideloading apps does not erase your data, but issues can occur if you use untrusted tools or apps.
  • Security: Only sideload apps from trusted sources. Sideloaded apps are not reviewed by Apple and could contain malware or security risks.
  • App revokes and limitations: Apps installed with a free Apple ID may expire after 7 days and need to be re-signed. Some sideloading methods may stop working after iOS updates.
  • Warranty: Sideloading alone does not void your warranty. However, using exploits or jailbreaking to enable sideloading may affect your warranty status.
