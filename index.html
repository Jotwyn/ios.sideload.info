What is Sideloading?

Sideloading is the process of installing apps on your iOS device from sources outside the official App Store. This allows you to use apps not available on the App Store, beta versions, or custom apps.

Disclaimer: Sideloading may void your warranty or violate Apple's terms of service. Proceed at your own risk.

Preparation

  1. Check your iOS version in Settings > General > About.
  2. Have a computer (Windows, macOS, or Linux) ready for most methods (unless using TrollStore or some eSigner apps).
  3. Backup your device using iCloud or iTunes/Finder before proceeding.
  4. Download the necessary tools for your iOS version and method (see below).

iOS 18 Sideloading & Jailbreak Methods

  • Jailbreak: No jailbreak is available for iOS 18 as of June 2024.
  • TrollStore: Not available on iOS 18.

AltStore (Recommended for Sideloading)

  1. Download AltServer:
    • Windows
    • macOS
  2. Windows Users: Install the iTunes Web version and iCloud Web version (not Microsoft Store versions):
    • iTunes Web (x64): Download
    • iTunes Web (x32): Download
    • iCloud Web: Download
  3. Install AltServer on your computer and follow the on-screen instructions.
  4. Connect your iOS device via USB and open AltServer.
  5. Click the AltServer icon in your system tray (Windows) or menu bar (macOS), select your device, and choose Install AltStore.
  6. Enter your Apple ID credentials when prompted (use a secondary Apple ID for safety).
  7. On your iOS device, open AltStore and sign in with the same Apple ID.
  8. To sideload an app:
    • Download the .ipa file you want to install (e.g., from iOS Ninja).
    • In AltStore, tap the + icon, select the .ipa file, and follow prompts to install.
  9. Apps installed with a free Apple ID expire after 7 days. Open AltStore and refresh them before expiry.

For more help, see the official AltStore website or AltStore Discord.

SideStore (Wireless Sideloading after Setup)

  1. First, install AltStore as above (SideStore requires AltServer for initial install).
  2. Download the latest SideStore IPA: sidestore.ipa
  3. On your computer, open AltServer, hold Shift (Windows) or Option (macOS), click the AltServer icon, and select Sideload .ipa. Choose the SideStore IPA.
  4. After installation, on your iOS device, go to Settings > General > VPN & Device Management and trust your Apple ID developer profile.
  5. Enable Developer Mode on your device: Settings > Privacy & Security > Developer Mode.
  6. Install StosVPN from the App Store: Download StosVPN. Enable the VPN when using SideStore.
  7. Open SideStore, sign in with your Apple ID, and follow the in-app instructions to finish setup.
  8. To sideload apps, use the + icon in SideStore and select your .ipa file.

For detailed pairing file instructions (required for wireless refresh), see the SideStore Wiki.
Need help? Join the SideStore Discord.

Sideloadly

  1. Download Sideloadly for your OS:
    • Windows x64
    • Windows x32
    • macOS
  2. Install the iTunes Web and iCloud Web versions (Windows only, see AltStore section above for links).
  3. Open Sideloadly, connect your device, and drag your .ipa file into the app.
  4. Enter your Apple ID credentials when prompted.
  5. Click Start and wait for the process to finish.
  6. On your device, go to Settings > General > VPN & Device Management and trust your Apple ID profile.

See Sideloadly website for troubleshooting and guides.

eSigner Apps (Esign, Scarlet, Bullfrog Assistant, etc)

  1. How eSigner Apps Work: These apps (like Esign, Scarlet, Bullfrog Assistant) let you install .ipa files directly on your device. They do not use your Apple ID for signing. Instead, they use enterprise or developer certificates, often from companies or resellers.
  2. Certificate Revokes: Free certificates are revoked by Apple frequently, causing apps to stop opening. Some eSigner apps offer "paid" or "private" certificates that last longer and may come with a revoke warranty or replacement.
  3. DNS Tricks: Some users use DNS services (like Larsenwork's iOS OCSP or NextDNS with OCSP blocking) to prevent iOS from checking if a certificate is revoked. This can keep apps working after a revoke, but is not 100% reliable and may have security/privacy tradeoffs.
  4. How to Use:
    1. Install the eSigner app (usually via an existing certificate, AltStore/SideStore, or TestFlight invite).
    2. Open the app and import your .ipa file (from Files, Safari, or direct download).
    3. Tap to sign and install. The app will use its certificate to sign and install the app.
    4. After installation, go to Settings > General > VPN & Device Management and trust the certificate profile.
  5. Risks: Never pay for "signing services" from random sellers. Only use trusted sources. Apps may stop working at any time if the certificate is revoked.

Xcode (macOS only)

  1. Download the app's source code (e.g., from GitHub) or .ipa file.
  2. Open Xcode, create a new project or open the source code.
  3. Connect your device and select it as the build target.
  4. Sign the app with your Apple ID (free or paid developer account).
  5. Build and run the app on your device.

See Apple's Xcode page for more info.

Follow the same steps as for iOS 18 above. Sideloadly works on iOS 17.

TrollStore (iOS 17.0 only)

  1. Check your exact iOS version in Settings > General > About. TrollStore only works on 17.0 (not 17.0.1+).
  2. Visit TrollStore for the latest instructions and supported devices.
  3. Download the appropriate TrollStore installer for your device (e.g., TrollInstallerX for A12+ devices).
  4. Follow the step-by-step guide on the TrollStore website to install and activate TrollStore.
  5. Once installed, open TrollStore and import your .ipa files for permanent installation.

TrollStore is not available on iOS 17.0.1 or later.

Troubleshooting

  • App won't open: Make sure you have trusted the developer profile in Settings > General > Device Management.
  • App expires after 7 days: Free Apple IDs require re-signing apps every 7 days. Paid developer accounts last 1 year.
  • Installation failed: Try restarting your device and computer, and ensure all software is up to date.
  • Apple ID issues: Use a secondary Apple ID for sideloading to avoid account lockouts.
  • Certificate revoked: If using an eSigner app, the app may stop working if Apple revokes the certificate. You can try DNS tricks (see eSigner section) to bypass revokes, but this is not always reliable.
  • TrollStore not working: Make sure your iOS version is supported (see TrollStore).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is sideloading safe?

Sideloading is generally safe if you trust the source of the app. Only sideload apps from reputable sources. Avoid pirated or cracked apps.

Can I sideload apps without a computer?

Yes, with methods like SideStore, eSigner apps (Esign, Scarlet, Bullfrog Assistant), and TrollStore (if supported). However, initial setup may require a computer.

Will sideloading void my warranty?

Sideloading itself does not void your warranty, but misuse or installing malicious apps can cause issues. Jailbreaking may void your warranty.

What is the difference between AltStore and SideStore?

AltStore requires a computer to refresh apps, while SideStore allows wireless sideloading after initial setup.

What is an eSigner app?

eSigner apps (like Esign, Scarlet, Bullfrog Assistant) let you sign and install .ipa files directly on your device using enterprise or developer certificates, not your Apple ID. These certificates can be revoked by Apple at any time, causing apps to stop working. Some DNS tricks can help bypass revokes, but are not always reliable.

What is TrollStore?

TrollStore is a permanent signing solution for certain iOS versions, allowing you to install apps without re-signing every 7 days. Not available on iOS 18 or most iOS 17.x.

Can I jailbreak iOS 18 or 17?

No jailbreak is available for iOS 18 or 17 as of now.
