What is Sideloading?

Sideloading is the process of installing apps on your iOS device from sources outside the official App Store. This allows you to use apps not available on the App Store, beta versions, or custom apps.

Disclaimer: Sideloading may void your warranty or violate Apple's terms of service. Proceed at your own risk.

Preparation

  1. Ensure your device is running a supported iOS version.
  2. Have a computer (Windows, macOS, or Linux) ready.
  3. Download the necessary tools (see below).
  4. Backup your device using iCloud or iTunes/Finder.

Sideloading Methods

1. Using AltStore

  • Download AltStore on your computer.
  • Install AltServer and follow the on-screen instructions.
  • Connect your iOS device via USB and install AltStore.
  • On your device, open AltStore and sign in with your Apple ID.
  • Download or import the .ipa file you wish to sideload.
  • Install the app via AltStore.

2. Using Sideloadly

  • Download Sideloadly for your OS.
  • Open Sideloadly and connect your device.
  • Drag and drop the .ipa file into Sideloadly.
  • Enter your Apple ID credentials when prompted.
  • Click "Start" and wait for the process to complete.
  • Trust the developer profile in Settings > General > Device Management.

3. Using Xcode (macOS only)

  • Download the app's source code or .ipa file.
  • Open Xcode and create a new project or open the source code.
  • Connect your device and select it as the build target.
  • Sign the app with your Apple ID (free or paid developer account).
  • Build and run the app on your device.

Troubleshooting

  • App won't open: Make sure you have trusted the developer profile in Settings > General > Device Management.
  • App expires after 7 days: Free Apple IDs require re-signing apps every 7 days. Paid developer accounts last 1 year.
  • Installation failed: Try restarting your device and computer, and ensure all software is up to date.
  • Apple ID issues: Use a secondary Apple ID for sideloading to avoid account lockouts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is sideloading safe?

Sideloading is generally safe if you trust the source of the app. Only sideload apps from reputable sources.

Can I sideload apps without a computer?

Some online services claim to offer this, but they are often unreliable or unsafe. Using a computer is recommended.

Will sideloading void my warranty?

Sideloading itself does not void your warranty, but misuse or installing malicious apps can cause issues.
