What is sideloading?

By default, Apple doesn’t allow installing applications outside of the App Store. Compared to other operating systems such as Android or Windows, they natively allow installing apps outside of the App Store. Apple doesn’t as it risks the privacy of their users, and also doesn’t want users accidentally installing apps outside of the App Store.

Is sideloading dangerous?

Sideloading itself isn’t necessarily dangerous as no viruses have been seen “in the wild”. However, it is important to know that spyware frameworks have been exploited previously, such as Pegasus or SeaShell.

As long as you are careful of the applications you install, there is almost no risk of installing a virus.

Is sideloading illegal?

It all comes down to what you sideload. Installing pirated apps or apps with infinite currency, etc, are considered illegal. But the chances of you getting arrested are extremely low, and if you were reported, you would only be fined.

Mass distribution of pirated applications is illegal and you probably will be arrested if caught.
